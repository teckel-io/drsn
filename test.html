Why Diagnostic Tests Matter

Advanced diagnostic testing is essential to accurately assess your oral health and identify potential problems early. At Delapre Dental Care, Dr Sabina Nasir uses the latest diagnostic tools to evaluate your teeth, gums, and overall dental condition.

Early detection of issues like tooth decay, gum disease, and oral infections allows us to provide prompt, targeted treatment, often preserving your natural teeth and preventing more serious complications.

Our Diagnostic Services

Digital Intraoral X-rays — High-resolution images of individual teeth and surrounding bone, taken with minimal radiation exposure. These images help identify decay between teeth, assess bone health, and evaluate the roots of teeth before treatment.

Orthopantomograms (OPGs) — Full-mouth panoramic X-ray images showing all teeth and both jawbones in a single image. OPGs are valuable for assessing tooth development, identifying missing teeth, and evaluating jaw structure.

Visual Oral Examination — A thorough clinical assessment by Dr Nasir, examining all tooth surfaces, gums, soft tissues, and the oral cavity for signs of decay, disease, or abnormality.

Gum Pocket Probing — Measurement of the space between teeth and gums to assess gum health and identify early signs of gum disease or periodontal disease.

Intraoral Camera Imaging — High-magnification digital photographs of specific areas of your mouth, allowing detailed visualisation of your dental condition and helping us explain findings clearly.

Some advanced diagnostic tests such as 3D CBCT (cone beam computed tomography) scans may be referred to a specialist radiography centre if clinically necessary.

When Tests Are Recommended

Diagnostic tests are typically recommended in the following situations:

Initial Dental Assessment — At your first visit, diagnostic testing establishes a baseline record of your oral health and helps Dr Nasir develop a personalised treatment plan.

Regular Check-ups — Periodic X-rays (usually annually for most patients, or as advised by Dr Nasir) monitor your teeth and gums over time and detect new problems at an early stage.

Suspected Problems — If you experience tooth pain, discomfort, or sensitivity, diagnostic tests help identify the underlying cause and guide appropriate treatment.

Pre-Treatment Planning — Before procedures such as fillings, root canal treatment, extractions, or implant placement, diagnostic imaging helps Dr Nasir plan treatment precisely and discuss options with you.

Monitoring Existing Conditions — If you have a history of gum disease, decay-prone areas, or previous dental treatment, periodic testing ensures any recurrence is identified promptly.

Radiation Safety

Modern digital X-ray systems use significantly less radiation than older film-based equipment. We follow strict ALARA principles (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) to minimise your radiation exposure whilst obtaining diagnostic-quality images.

Your safety is a priority. Protective lead aprons are provided during X-ray procedures, and tests are only recommended when clinically justified and likely to influence your treatment plan. If you are pregnant or suspect you may be pregnant, please inform Dr Nasir before any X-rays are taken.

We do not routinely X-ray all patients; X-rays are prescribed on an individual basis according to clinical need and your personal risk profile.
