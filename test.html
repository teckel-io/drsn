Why Professional Dental Tests Matter

Regular dental tests and assessments form the foundation of preventive care. Professional diagnostic procedures allow Dr Sabina Nasir to identify potential problems in their earliest stages — often before you experience any symptoms or pain.

Early detection means simpler, more affordable treatment and better long-term outcomes for your oral health. Whether you visit us for routine check-ups or have specific concerns, our comprehensive assessment protocols ensure nothing is overlooked.

Our Comprehensive Test Suite
Oral Health Assessment
A comprehensive visual and tactile examination of your teeth, gums, tongue, and all soft tissues inside your mouth. This is the foundation of all dental care and screening.
What to expect: Dr Nasir will examine your mouth thoroughly, checking for cavities, gum disease, oral cancer risk factors, and other abnormalities. The assessment typically takes 15–20 minutes for a new patient, 10–15 minutes for established patients.
Digital X-Ray Imaging
Modern, low-dose radiographic imaging that provides detailed views of tooth structures, bone levels, and root systems. Digital X-rays deliver significantly lower radiation than traditional film-based radiography.
What to expect: A small sensor is placed in your mouth to capture images of different areas. The process is quick, painless, and completely safe. Digital images appear on screen immediately, allowing Dr Nasir to discuss findings with you in real time.
Periodontal Assessment
Specialised evaluation of your gum health, including pocket depth measurement, bleeding assessment, and detection of gum recession or bone loss associated with periodontal disease.
What to expect: A fine probe is gently used to measure the space between teeth and gums. Dr Nasir will also assess your gums for signs of inflammation, bleeding, or infection. This assessment is painless and takes 10–15 minutes.
Plaque & Calculus Evaluation
Assessment of bacterial plaque biofilm accumulation and hardened calculus (tartar) deposits on your teeth. Both plaque and calculus are associated with tooth decay and gum disease.
What to expect: Dr Nasir will identify areas where plaque or calculus have built up. Professional cleaning can remove calculus, and Dr Nasir will provide personalised advice on plaque control through improved brushing and flossing technique.
Cavity Risk Assessment
Personalised evaluation of your individual decay risk based on oral hygiene habits, dietary factors, saliva flow, and family history. This allows Dr Nasir to recommend preventive strategies tailored to your specific needs.
What to expect: Dr Nasir will discuss your diet, brushing and flossing routine, and any symptoms you have noticed. Risk factors are identified and preventive recommendations are provided. Higher-risk patients may be offered more frequent appointments or specialist preventive treatments.
What Happens During Your Assessment

A comprehensive dental assessment appointment at Delapre Dental Care typically follows this structure:

  • Medical and dental history review: Dr Nasir will discuss any previous dental work, current health conditions, medications, and any specific concerns you have about your oral health.
  • Visual examination: A careful inspection of your teeth, gums, tongue, and soft tissues, checking for cavities, gum disease, wear patterns, and other abnormalities.
  • Radiographic imaging (if appropriate): Digital X-rays may be taken to visualise tooth structures, bone levels, and detect problems not visible to the naked eye.
  • Specialised assessments: Depending on your individual circumstances, assessments such as periodontal evaluation or cavity risk analysis may be performed.
  • Discussion of findings: Dr Nasir will explain all findings clearly, answer your questions, and discuss any treatment recommendations or preventive strategies.
  • Treatment planning: If any treatment is necessary, a detailed plan and cost estimate will be provided before any work commences.
  • Follow-up scheduling: Your next appointment will be arranged based on your individual needs — typically 6 months for routine maintenance, or sooner if specific concerns are identified.
Benefits of Regular Assessment

Patients who attend regular professional assessments enjoy significant advantages:

  • Early problem detection: Cavities, gum disease, and other issues are identified in their earliest, most treatable stages — often before they cause pain or require complex intervention.
  • Preventive confidence: You know exactly what your oral health status is and what you can do to maintain or improve it. No surprises.
  • Cost savings: Early treatment is always simpler and more affordable than emergency treatment for advanced problems.
  • Improved general health: Oral health is closely linked to overall health. Regular dental care contributes to systemic wellbeing.
  • Personalised guidance: Dr Nasir provides tailored advice specific to your individual risk factors and circumstances, not generic recommendations.
  • Professional cleaning: Regular professional cleaning removes tartar and plaque that home care cannot eliminate, protecting your teeth and gums.
